1. Historical Context of Cannabis Consumption

  • Ancient use:
    • Dates back to 3rd millennium BC. but could go as far as 8000 BC (Pre-Neolithic)
    • Uses: fiber, food, medicine, and psychoactive properties.
  • Middle Eastern Use:
    • Ancient Israelites (8th century BCE): Evidence of cannabis in religious rituals.
    • Napoleon’s Campaign in Egypt: French soldiers used hashish, leading to its introduction in France.

2. Cannabis Use in France (2023)

  • Statistics:
    • 10.8% of French adults (18-64) have used cannabis in the past year.
    • Smoking remains the main method, but vaporization is growing among health-conscious users.

3. Production Method: Smoke vs. Vapor

  • Smoke:
    • Created by combustion at high temperatures (600°C - 900°C).
    • Contains THC/CBD but also 150+ harmful chemicals: tar, benzene, PAHs (carcinogens).
  • Vapor:
    • Created by heating (160°C - 230°C) without burning.
    • Less toxic: Vapor contains 36%-61% THC and far fewer non-cannabinoid compounds.

4. Popular Vaporizer Models

  • Volcano: Desktop model, known for precision and convection heating.
  • Crafty: Portable, hybrid heating with excellent temperature control.

5. Cannabis Use Prevention

  • Health risks remain, particularly for youth and those with mental health vulnerabilities.
  • Prevention programs: Encourage responsible use and informed choices.
  • Open discussion about risks and benefits is essential.

Conclusion

  • Vaporization: Cleaner alternative, less exposure to toxins.
  • Awareness and responsibility are key to safe cannabis use.
